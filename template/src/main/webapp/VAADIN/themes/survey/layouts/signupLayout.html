Anonymity Agreement

It is important for the integrity of this experiment that all participants remain anonymous to other participants and to the researchers. All information collected during the experiment is stored with reference to a random id. This random id is not connected in any way to your email.

However, there are many $20 cash prizes to be earned in this experiment. We will use your email to contact you, should you win. And you email will be used to send a reminder about the experiment. After the experiment is over, all email addresses will be deleted.

To emphasize:

All information you provide during the experiment is not connected to your email. This includes answers to questions, and your behavior during the simulation.

It is important that you understand that all information recorded about your behavior is completely anonymous.

Consent Form

You are invited to participate in a research project entitled “A Simulation Between Team Members.” Please read this form carefully, and feel free to ask any questions you might have.

Researcher: Dr. Christopher Poile, Assistant Professor, Department of Industrial Relations and Organizational Behavior, Edwards School of Business, 966-2491, poile@edwards.usask.ca

Co-Researcher: Dr. Matthias Spitzmuller, Assistant Professor, National University of Singapore, Department of Management & Organisation, Phone: +65 6516 7230. bizms@nus.edu.sg

The purpose of the study to is to examine the behavior of individuals as they participate in small work groups. Participants will be asked to fill out a pre-task questionnaire. Participants will be anonymously paired with another person to play a game that simulates a simple work task. During the game and after the game you will be asked to fill out a very short set of questions. The study will take between 25 minutes and an hour, depending on the speed of the participants. The results of this study will be used in writing an academic research paper and may be presented at academic conferences and/or published in journals. Responses will be aggregated across all respondents and only averages will be reported. There is no identifying information recorded at any time. Your behavior is anonymous.

Your participation is voluntary, and you need answer only those questions that you are comfortable with. There is no guarantee that you will personally benefit from your involvement. The information that is shared will be held in strict confidence, accessible only to the principal researcher. Experimental data will be stored by the research supervisor for a minimum of five years. If the supervisor chooses to destroy the data after the five years, it will be destroyed beyond recovery. You may withdraw from the research project for any reason, at any time, without penalty of any sort and still receive the participation marks. If you withdraw from the research project at any time, any data that you have contributed will be destroyed at your request. Your right to withdraw data from the study will apply until the data has been pooled. After this it is possible that some form of research dissemination will have already occurred and it may not be possible to withdraw your data.

If you have any questions concerning the research project, please feel free to ask at any point; you are also free to contact the researchers at the numbers provided if you have other questions. This research project has been approved on ethical grounds by the University of Saskatchewan Behavioural Research Ethics Board on Dec 21 2011. Any questions regarding your rights as a participant may be addressed to that committee through the Ethics Office (966-2084). Out of town participants may call collect.

The principle researcher will send an email out to all participants of the experiment explaining the experiment after it is completed. Any questions you may have can be addressed over email or in person.
